Conference Tracks

Click on a track to view its description and sub-themes. These tracks are indicative only and not strictly limited.

Track 1: Health Risk and Financing

Health risk refers to the potential for adverse health outcomes and financial burdens due to illness or injury. Health financing is essential for achieving universal health coverage, involving revenue raising, pooling funds, and purchasing services. Risk financing focuses on how health entities manage and pay for these risks effectively, utilizing methods like insurance and risk-sharing. Ultimately, financial risk protection ensures individuals can access necessary health services without facing catastrophic costs.

Sub-themes:

  • Equity in health financing and risk protection
  • Health Insurance and risk protection
  • Technology and health financing
  • Monitoring and Evaluation of Health Financing Policies
  • Gender and Health Financing
  • Health Financing Reforms
  • Universal Health Coverage and Insurance Accessibility
  • Behavioural Economics in Health Insurance Choice
  • Global Perspective on Health Insurance Financing

Track 2: Occupational Health

Occupational health is crucial for ensuring worker well-being, productivity, and economic sustainability. The economic burden of occupational diseases and injuries poses significant challenges, affecting both workers and industries. Effective policy analysis can drive interventions that enhance workplace safety, especially in the informal sector and among vulnerable groups. Strengthening occupational health frameworks contributes to sustainable development, mental well-being, and resilience in times of crisis.

Sub-themes:

  • Economic Burden of Occupational Diseases and Injuries
  • Policy Analysis in Occupational Health
  • Occupational Health and Sustainable Development
  • Mental Health in the Workplace
  • Informal Sector and Occupational Health Financing
  • Aging Workforce, Gender and Occupational Health
  • Economic Resilience in Workplace Health during Pandemics
  • Health State Dependence

Track 3: Climate Change, Environmental Governance, and Health

Climate change and environmental degradation pose significant health risks, affecting vulnerable populations disproportionately. Rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and pollution contribute to the spread of infectious diseases, respiratory issues, and mental health concerns. Environmental governance frameworks, such as DeGrowth, LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment), Green Transition, and Green Recovery, aim to mitigate these impacts by promoting sustainable policies and responsible resource use. Addressing these challenges requires a multidimensional approach that integrates climate resilience, gender-sensitive health policies, and international collaboration to ensure equitable health outcomes.

Sub-themes:

  • Climate Change, Energy Poverty, and Health Inequalities
  • Gender, Environment, and Health: Intersectional Perspectives
  • Environmental Governance and Public Health: Policy and Practice
  • Green Transitions and Their Impact on Health Systems
  • Health Risks of Air Pollution and Industrialization
  • Climate Resilience and Community Health Adaptation
  • The Role of Global Institutions in Health and Environmental Governance
  • Behavioral Responses to Climate-Induced Health Risks
  • Equity and Justice in Climate Change and Health Policies

Track 4: Health System, Institutions and Governance

This call for papers seeks contributions examining the critical dimensions of health system governance, policy, and institutional frameworks, emphasizing their role in shaping health policy and service delivery. We invite analyses of national and sub-national governance models, policy implementation challenges, and innovative strategies for strengthening health systems. A key focus is health system responsiveness, which ensures that healthcare services meet individuals' needs efficiently and equitably. Papers exploring patient-centered care, community engagement, and accountability mechanisms, along with studies on disparities in responsiveness and policy interventions for improvement, are particularly welcome. Another essential aspect is health literacy at both individual and organizational levels, which significantly influences health outcomes. Contributions may examine how individuals access health information, navigate healthcare systems, and make informed health decisions, as well as how organizations can promote health literacy through digital health tools, communication strategies, and policy interventions.

Sub-themes:

  • Health governance, policy and institutional frameworks for health system
  • Health system responsiveness
  • Health literacy: Individual and organizational

Track 5: Institutions and Injustice: Reimagining Systems of Care and Healing

This track explores the critical issues surrounding women’s reproductive health and child well-being, focusing on the socio-economic, cultural, and systemic factors that influence health outcomes. It invites discussions on topics such as maternal and child nutrition, reproductive rights, justice, and autonomy, as well as innovations and policy reforms aimed at reducing inequalities and improving access to quality healthcare for women and children.

Sub-themes:

  • Maternal Health and Safe Motherhood
  • Child Health and Nutrition
  • Quality of care for Maternal and Child Health
  • Impact of Climate Change on Women and Child Health
  • Technology and Innovation in Maternal and Child Health
  • Family Planning and Contraceptive Access
  • Reproductive Justice and Autonomy
  • Inequities in Fertility and Reproductive Health

Track 6: Health Behaviour and Cognition

Addressing the economic dimensions of health behaviors among the vulnerable population is imperative for creating resilient health systems and societies. This conference will serve as a platform for interdisciplinary dialogue and collaboration, driving forward evidence-based solutions to reduce the burden of health through economically sound strategies.

Sub-themes:

  • Exploring Health Behaviours among the Ageing Population
  • Health Behaviours and Women
  • Health Behaviours and Non-communicable Diseases
  • Cognitive Health

Track 7: Gender Inequality in Health and Development

Gender inequality in health and development remains a significant barrier to achieving equitable well-being worldwide. Women often face disparities in access to healthcare, nutrition, and economic opportunities, leading to poorer health outcomes. Structural factors such as discriminatory social norms, unequal resource distribution, and limited decision-making power exacerbate these challenges. Addressing gender-based inequities requires targeted policies, inclusive healthcare systems, and empowerment initiatives that ensure equal opportunities for all, ultimately fostering sustainable development and improved public health.

Sub-themes:

  • Disparities in Healthcare Access and Utilization
  • Economic and Social Determinants of Health Inequities
  • Gendered Burden of Non-Communicable and Communicable Diseases
  • Violence, Mental Health, and Well-being
  • Health Insurance and Financial Protection Gaps
  • Policy, Governance, and Gender-Sensitive Health Systems

Track 8: Economic, Demographic, Social, Medical and Public Policy Perspectives on Aging, Health, and Wellness

Aging is a global challenge with significant economic, social, and policy implications, requiring societies to reassess healthcare, legal rights, housing, and social services while promoting society’s engagement and inclusivity. Medical advancements continue to redefine aging management, while social and cultural perspectives shape family roles, community support, and end-of-life care. On a personal level, aging brings shifts in relationships, independence, and living arrangements, necessitating adaptation and comprehensive support systems.

Sub-themes:

  • Economic status and the wellbeing of older adults
  • Increase in the demand and cost of healthcare for older adults
  • Public health and healthcare policies related to aging
  • Aging-friendly access to public healthcare facilities and other public goods
  • Food and nutrition needs for the aging population
  • Management of aging-related communicable and non-communicable diseases
  • Social support and networks
  • ICT impact on aging
  • Physical and mental wellbeing of aging population
  • Lifestyles and qualities of life for older adults
  • Time activities utilization of aging population
  • The economic and societal impacts of aged population

Track 9: Enhancing Healthcare Efficiency and Productivity

Healthcare efficiency is crucial in optimizing resource use while ensuring quality care delivery. Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA) provides a powerful tool for evaluating efficiency in healthcare systems, financing mechanisms, and risk management. By assessing productivity, benchmarking performance, and guiding policy improvements, DEA helps stakeholders make data-driven decisions. Integrating DEA in health financing ensures cost-effective healthcare delivery, equitable resource distribution, and financial risk protection, promoting universal health coverage.

Sub-themes:

  • Efficiency and Performance Measurement in Healthcare
  • Efficiency and Productivity in Health Financing
  • Healthcare Resource Allocation and Input-Output Optimization
  • Productivity Change over Time: Malmquist and TFP Indices in Health
  • Equity-Adjusted Efficiency Analysis in Healthcare Delivery

Track 10: Technological Disruptions and Healthcare

Global healthcare is undergoing a revolution due to the intervention of information and communication technology (ICT). This track focuses on improving patient-centred digital health, closing the digital divide in healthcare, and using digital tools to deliver services more effectively. Additionally, how digitization improves distant care, increases patient autonomy, and changes public health outcomes will be discussed. The regulatory environment and compliance issues in digital healthcare will also be explored.

Sub-themes:

  • Digital divide in healthcare
  • Patient-centred digital health
  • Public health transformation through digital health
  • Digitization to enhance patient autonomy and improve remote care
  • Utilizing digital tools for efficient healthcare delivery and better outcomes
  • Regulations and compliance requirements for digital healthcare

Track 11: Economics, Nutrition, and Human Capital

Early age nutrition is an important marker of lifelong health, education, labour market, and other socioeconomic outcomes. As we approach the final year of the UN Decade of Action on Nutrition 2016–2025, it is crucial to assess progress made and explore emerging nutritional challenges for policymakers in both developing and developed countries.

Sub-themes:

  • Child (mal)nutrition in LMICs
  • Nutrition transition
  • Double burden of malnutrition
  • Nutritional outcomes and human capital
  • Political economy of health and nutrition

Track 12: Development Disruptions: Education, Employment, Labour & Migration, and Inequality

Development processes around the world are being reshaped by rapid and overlapping disruptions in education systems, labour markets, migration flows, and social structures. Economic uncertainty, climate change, technological transformation, and the long-term effects of the COVID-19 pandemic have intensified vulnerabilities, especially among youth, informal workers, migrants, and marginalized communities. This track focuses on the structural challenges and inequalities emerging from these disruptions and their implications for inclusive development.

Sub-themes:

  • Disruptions in Education and Human Capital Development
  • Labour Market Transformations and Informality
  • Migration, Displacement, and Development Gaps
  • Technology, Employment, and the Future of Work
  • Youth, Skills, and Employment Transitions
  • Social Protection and Labour Rights in Precarious Economies
  • Structural Inequality: Caste, Gender, Region, and Class in Development Outcomes

Track 13: Digital Humanities

This track invites explorations into how digital methods transform traditional humanities research, pedagogy, and public engagement. From digital archives and data visualization to algorithmic critique and participatory platforms, the track encourages contributions that interrogate the possibilities and challenges of digitizing human experience, memory, and cultural heritage in the 21st century.

Sub-themes:

  • Digital Archiving, Preservation, and Cultural Memory
  • Text Mining, Corpus Analysis, and Computational Linguistics
  • Digital Pedagogy and Humanities Education
  • Visualization and Mapping in Humanities Research
  • Algorithmic Bias, Ethics, and Critical Digital Studies
  • Public Humanities and Digital Storytelling
  • Interdisciplinary Innovations: Art, AI, and Emerging Technologies
